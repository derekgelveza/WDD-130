Home page

Introduction and Purpose

This Website serves as a professional digital portfolio that highlights my personal and academic jounrey in th efield of technology and develpoment. It is designed not only to showcase what I've accomplished so far but also to demonstrate how I apply what I learn to real-world projects. By creating this site, I also am demonstrating what I have learned during the semester regarding web development, content organization, and perosnal branding-which are skills that are needed in the job market

Target Audience

The primary audieence for this website are recruiters and hiring managers who are looking to see my technical skills and project experience.

Why People Will Visit

People will ocme to this website to evaluate my qualifications, learn about my technical abilites, and see real exampels of how I've applied what I've learned. Recruiters and hiring managers will visit to determine if my skills would be applicable to the real world.

Questions Visitors Will Want Answered

Visitors will want to know:What coding languages do I know? What kind of projects have I worked on? What role did I play in each project? Do I have the experience they're looking for a candidate or collaborator? They may also be interested in how I think, Solve problems, and communicate - things they can infer from my writing and code samples

Projects

Project Showcase Overview

This section of the website will feature a collection of my projects, categorized by type (personal, academic, or collaborative). Each project will have a dedicated summary page with screenshots, code snippets, and the outcome. This helps visitors assess my experience and approach to problem-solving.

Skill Highlights and Tools

Visitors to this child page will see a breakdown of the techincal skills I've developed, such as programming languages, frameworks, databses, and development tools. These will be presented with short explinations and links to projects where each skill was applied. This structure allows recruiters to confirm whether I match the skill set required for specific roles

Learning in Action

I want to emphasize that I am a continous learner. This paragraph will describe how I tackle new concepts, and work through challenges in a project. I'll share exampels where learning a new coding langauge helped me grow as a developer. This shows not just what I know, but how I learn and adapt.

Project Navigation and Interactivity

Each project entry will offer links to the live project (if available), Github, and written documentation. This way, visitors can choose to explore my work in the way that suites them-quick overviews or deep dives. The design will support skimming as well as detailed browsing

Resume & Contact

Resume Access

This page will feature a downloadable PDF of my resume as well as an HTML version for easy viewing. It will be kept updated regularly. Key areas of the resume -like education, experience, and certifications-will be emphasized visually to help recruiters find what they need quickly

Contact & Networking

Visitors will find ways to contact me: an embedded contact form, email link, and links to my Github. A simple, clean layout will ensure that reaching out is easy. I want to create a low-friction path for someone to connect with me

Why This Site Instead of Another?

Every piece of content is personalized and reflective of who I am as a developer. Instead of just listing skills and titles, I show how I use them and what I've learned. It's meant to make a strong impression.
